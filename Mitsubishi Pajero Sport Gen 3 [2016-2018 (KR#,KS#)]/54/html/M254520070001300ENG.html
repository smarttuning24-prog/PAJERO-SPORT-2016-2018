SYSTEM OPERATION


Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System function


caution
  • The Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System does not operate in the following conditions:

    1. The ignition switch is other than ON.
    2. The Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System is set to OFF by operating the Forward Collision Mitigation System (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch.
    3. The FCM and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System OFF indicator lights up.
    4. The selector lever is set to the "P" (PARK) or "N" (NEUTRAL) position.
  • It sometimes happens that a motorcycle, bicycle or pedestrian are detected as obstacles, but these are not the object of the Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System operation.
  • The Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System may not operate in the following conditions.

    1. When there is an extremely change in the outside temperature.
    2. Under adverse weather conditions (rain, strong winds, snow, sand storms, etc.).
    3. When approaching an obstacle too closely.
    4. When a vehicle cuts in front or behind your vehicle very closely.
    5. When only a part of the obstacle is within the detection areas of the sensor.
    6. When the rain, snow, water or dirt adheres to the sensor.
    7. When the sensor is extremely hot or cold (while the vehicle is parked for a long period of time under a blazing sun or in cold weather).
    8. Immediately after the engine starting.
    9. When driving on a road with many and successive curves, including when passing their entrances and outlets.
    10. When driving on a road with steep and alternating up and down slopes.
    11. When a road surface is surging, and there is unevenness or gravel.
    12. When the system judges driver’s steering or gear shifting actions as evasive actions to avoid collision.
    13. For a short period after the system judges the repeated depression of the accelerator pedal at a few seconds interval as a release operation.
    14. When the vehicle body is extremely inclined by carrying heavy loads or improper adjustment of the tyre pressure.
    15. When the driving is unstable due to an accident or trouble.
    16. The system receives ultrasonic noise from other source (the horns of other vehicles, motorcycle engines, brakes, radios, pouring rain, splashing water, snow traction device (tyre chains), etc.).
    17. When the obstacle is not horizontal to the ground or not perpendicular to the vehicle traveling direction.
    18. The sensors or surroundings have been wiped by hand, or stickers or accessories have been attached.
  • The Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System operation can be cancelled when the system judges the handle operation as evasive actions or when the obstacle moves out of the detectable area of the sensor.
  • The Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System may not operate because the sensor cannot detect the vehicle or obstacle as follows.

    1. Vehicle towing a trailer etc.
    2. A freight trailer that is not carrying a container.
    3. Vehicle that has a protruding load from the carrier.
    4. Obstacles in low positions.
    5. Vehicle that is extremely stained.
    6. Vehicle or obstacle that is covered with snow.
    7. A large mesh screen fence.
    8. The vehicle is the specific shape such as the carrier car.
    9. Obstacles that have irregular surface.
    10. Obstacles that have the shape of the pole such as the road marker or the streetlight.
    11. Obstacles that exist in the high position from the ground.
    12. Obstacles that are soft and absorb ultrasonic easily, such as spongy material or snow.
    13. Obstacles that are shaped with a sharp angle.
    14. A wall surface is not flat.
  • The Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System may be triggered under the following situations.

    1. When the surrounding vehicle or oncoming vehicle drives blowing up the water, snow or sand on the road.
    2. When the rain, snow, water or dirt adheres to the sensor.
    3. When there are objects, steps or projections on the road surface.
    4. When the parking gate or railway barrier is raised imperfectly.
    5. When running the road, the gradient changes suddenly.
    6. When passing through the mass of the steam, fog.
    7. When passing close to the vehicle or an obstacle.
    8. When passing through a vinyl curtain etc.
    9. When the surrounding area is overgrown with weeds.
    10. When leaving from a parallel parking.
    11. The system receives ultrasonic noise from other sources (the horns of other vehicles, motorcycle engines, brakes, radios, pouring rain, splashing water, snow traction device (tyre chains), etc.).
  • Turn off the system by pressing the Forward Collision Mitigation System (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch beforehand when the vehicle is placed in any of the following situations as the system can operate unexpectedly.

    1. When using an automatic car wash.
    2. When returning the fallen wheel in a ditch or escaping from mud.
    3. When using a vehicle elevator or a Multi-storey car park.
    4. Do not modify the suspension of your vehicle.
    5. When your vehicle’s wheels are driven by the engine on a lift.
    6. When your vehicle is towed or your vehicle tows another vehicle.
    7. When your vehicle is carried on a truck.
    8. When enjoying sports driving on a circuit.
    9. When your vehicle is on a chassis dynamometer or free rollers.
    10. When the tyre pressure is not proper.
    11. When tyre chains are attached.

Operation


The Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System function mitigates a collision injury by avoiding abrupt vehicle start and suppressing the engine output when the sensor detects another vehicle or an obstacle ahead or behind (within approximately 4 m or less) with your vehicle stationary or at low speed (approximately 10 km/h or less) and the steering wheel not turned extremely. When this system is triggered, an LCD display in the combination meter and a buzzer warns the driver visually and aurally.
note
  • When the towing bar mode is set by the corner sensor function, the area, which the sensor does not sense at the rear of the vehicle, is the same as for the activated corner sensor function. (Refer to "Corner sensor function operation")
  • If the UMS condition, which is related to an abnormal accelerator pedal operation, is satisfied three times within two seconds each, the third triggering will be rejected. Then the system will be disabled for 30 seconds.


Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System indication on the combination meter (multi information display), forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System OFF indication on the combination meter (multi information display), and a buzzer




When the system detects an obstacle in the running direction of the vehicle and recognises an abnormal accelerator pedal depression at vehicle start or low speed, it informs the driver that the Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System function has been activated. This is achieved visually and aurally by using the multi information display and the buzzer integrated in the clearance warning control unit. When the system detects an intermittent critical error temporarily due to an abnormal sensor signal, the system alerts the driver that the UMS is disabled temporarily. When the system detects an continuous critical error, the system alerts the driver that the UMS is disabled forcibly. When the system is switched off by using the forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch, this information is also displayed.

Clearance warning control unit


The clearance warning control unit calculates the distance to an obstacle by using ultrasonic sensors when the vehicle is driven forwards or rearwards (reversed). Then the control unit activates the Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System indication, the corner sensor system indication on the multi-information display in the combination meter and the buzzer (incorporated in the clearance warning control unit).

Corner sensor system function


Operation


The corner sensor system function measures the distance to the obstacle around your vehicle. Depending on the distance, the system warns the driver visually and aurally by using the display in the combination meter and the buzzer.


THE TOWING BAR MODE SWITCHING


By operating the sonar switch, "TOWING BAR MODE" and "STANDARD MODE" can be changed.

  1. Turn the ignition switch to the ON position.
  2. Move the shift lever or selector lever to the "R" (reverse) position.
  3. Turn off the corner sensor system by operating the sonar switch.(Corner sensor indicator: OFF)
  4. Press and hold the sonar switch for approximately 5 seconds.
  5. The buzzer* sounds and the mode is switched between "TOWING BAR MODE" and "STANDARD MODE".
note *: Sounds twice when the mode is switched to "TOWING BAR MODE". Sounds once when the mode is switched to "STANDARD MODE".

When there is an obstacle at a corner of the vehicle, the direction to the obstacle is displayed by the corner sensor indicator on the multi information display of the combination meter, and then the buzzer integrated in the clearance warning control unit sounds.

Blind spot warning


Operation


The blind spot warning system is activated when the blind spot warning switch is on, the selector lever is at other than P, N, R, and the vehicle speed is between 20 km/h and 140 km/h. Then four side sensors (two on the front bumper, two on the rear bumper) are initiated to detect a vehicle.

Forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch




The forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch is located at the left side of the instrument panel lower. You can activate and deactivate the FCM and the UMS by operating this switch. To activate (or trigger) the UMS, you should activate (or trigger) the FCM.
note When the ignition switch is switched on, the FCM and the UMS will be switched on automatically.
Switching off the system
  • When you press the forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch for at least three seconds while the FCM and the UMS are turned on, "forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System OFF" and "forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System OFF" will appear on the LCD display in the combination meter. Then the FCM and the UMS will be switched off.
Switching on the system
  • When you press the forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch for at least three seconds while the FCM and the UMS are switched off, "forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System OFF" will disappear on the LCD display in the combination meter. Then the FCM and the UMS will be switched on.

Sonar switch




The sonar switch is located at the left side of the instrument panel lower. You can switch on and off the corner sensor function by operating this switch.

Blind spot warning switch




The blind spot warning switch is located on the instrument panel lower cover. The driver can activate and deactivate the system by operating this switch.

Fail-safe and diagnosis functions


The clearance warning control unit always monitors input and output signals. When the system detects any malfunction, it will display a relevant message on the combination meter to alert the driver and disable the UMS, corner sensor and blind spot warning functions if necessary.
In addition, the clearance warning control unit has the following functions to facilitate system checks:

  • Setting a diagnosis code
  • Displaying service data
You can read the all items above by using the M.U.T.-III.
note On the M.U.T.-III, the Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System/corner sensor system/blind spot warning is referred to as "CORNER SENSOR/SONAR".

CODING FUNCTION
