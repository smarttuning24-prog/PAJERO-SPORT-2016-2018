ASTC-ECU


This ECU incorporates the ABS function, an EBD function, a HSA function, stability control function, ATC function, a brake assist control, a trailer stability assist control, a HDC function and an offroad traction control.
The hydraulic units of the ASC and ATC systems employ the automatic pressurisation function. These systems also incorporates a G & yaw rate sensor (integrated with ASTC-ECU), a steering wheel sensor, and a master cylinder pressure sensor (integrated with the hydraulic unit).

ACTIVE STABILITY & TRACTION CONTROL SYSTEM (ASTC)
The ASTC-ECU detects the vehicle movement based on the information received from each sensor and calculates ideal vehicle movement model. Based on the comparison of two results, yaw moment of the vehicle is generated by controlling braking force applied to certain wheels and understeer/oversteer is suppressed by controlling the engine output so that the vehicle movement can become similar to the calculated model.

ASTC function display

When the ASTC-ECU applies braking to a road wheel, an corresponding symbol on the drive-mode indicator flashes.
note To make the ASTC function display appear, you should operate the multi-information display switch to show "2WD/4WD operation indicator display" on the multi-information display.

Example of Active Stability Control operation




The Stability Control manages the vehicle attitude by creating a yaw moment from altering the balance between the cornering force and each wheel’s braking mechanism.

Example of the effect of control




For example, on a slippery surface, if the vehicle tends to be under-steered contrary to the driver’s intention, a yaw moment (a rotational moment) is created to restrain the under-steering by increasing the rear-inside braking force. On the other hand, when the vehicle tends to be oversteered, a yaw moment (a restorative moment) is created to restrain the oversteering by increasing the front-outside wheel braking force. Furthermore, when it is determined that the vehicle is over-speeding, safe and stable cornering is enabled by deceleration from reducing the engine output.

Cooperative control


Active stability control system transmits and receives data receives data required for controlling ABS and active traction control system so that cooperative control can be achieved.

Example of active traction control system control




The active traction control system automatically applies braking force to the freewheeling drive wheels caused by driving on the partially low-friction efficiency road surface and improper contact of wheels with the road so that loss of driving power caused by the freewheeling drive wheels can be prevented.
For example, when the vehicle is driving off road, application of braking force to the freewheeling front left and rear right wheels can give driving power to not freewheeling front right and rear left wheels so that evacuation capability equivalent to differential lock can be provided.

Hill START ASSIST (HSA) FUNCTION


A Hill Start Assist (HSA) will help the driver operate the braking when he/she starts and stops the vehicle on a steep slope. (This system operates only during uphill driving forwards or backwards).
System Configuration

When the vehicle has been completely stopped on a slope by the service brake, the ASTC-ECU judges whether the HSA function control should be activated in accordance with the signals (combination meter, ETACS-ECU, A/T-ECU, G and yaw rate sensor, master cylinder pressure sensor, engine-ECU, wheel speed sensor).

HSA function flow chart




note
  • The ASTC-ECU judges the inclination angle of the slope according to the signal sent by the G and yaw rate sensor.
  • When either of the following conditions is satisfied, the mode is shifted to the forcible termination mode, and HSA function control is terminated.

    • The parking brake operation is detected.
    • The system detects that the shift lever is moved to any other shift position than the uphill driving (Forward uphill driving: shift position other than "D", Reverse uphill driving: shift position other than "R").
    • The accelerator pedal and brake pedal are depressed at the same time.
    • The wheel speed sensor pulse is generated (when the vehicle moves)
    • The engine is stopped by engine stall, etc.
HSA function control activated status
  • When the ASTC-ECU judges that HSA function operation conditions are satisfied, the system will enter the HSA function control activated status. When the driver releases the brake pedal with the HSA function control activated, the system allows the hydraulic unit (incorporating the ASTC-ECU) to hold the brake wheel cylinder pressure.
Fluid pressure hold mode
  • The cut valve in the hydraulic unit (incorporating the ASTC-ECU) is closed by the brake fluid pressure due to the depression of the brake pedal. This causes the brake wheel cylinder pressure to be retained after the brake pedal is released, thus preventing the vehicle from moving backwards.
Start mode
  • The hydraulic unit (incorporating the ASTC-ECU) releases the brake wheel cylinder pressure maintained by the cut valve, depending on the engine torque generated by depressing the accelerator pedal.
Forcible termination mode
  • The system releases the brake wheel cylinder pressure, which the cut valve in the hydraulic unit (incorporating the ASTC-ECU) maintains, immediately. Then the hydraulic unit will terminates the HSA function control.

BRAKE ASSIST (BA) CONTROL


Operation description



  • If an emergency brake is determined by the brake pedal depression speed and force, the brake assist works to increase the braking force the drivers produce. [The braking force is not increased more than the brake performance (the friction force between the road surface and the tyres).]
  • The brake assist is completed when the brake pedal is released or the vehicle is stopped.

fail-safe function


The brake assist mechanism does not work when the ASTC indicator lamp illuminates.

TRAILER STABILITY ASSIST (TSA) CONTROL


When the system detects an unstable rolling, which is caused by the trailer vehicle during towing, the system controls the engine output and the road wheel brakes to stabilise the vehicle attitude.

System Configuration




The ASTC-ECU judges whether the TSA function should be activated according to the signals (G &yaw rate sensor, steering angular velocity sensor and wheel speed sensor) when a rolling is caused by the trailer vehicle.
The system illuminates the stop lamps automatically when the TSA control is activated.

Summary of TSA operation


When the ASTC-ECU uses various sensor signals to detect an rolling, which is caused by the trailer vehicle during towing, the system controls the engine output and the road wheel brakes to stabilise the vehicle attitude.
When the TSA function activates the brake control, the stop lamps illuminate.

ESS FUNCTION


ESS SYSTEM CONFIGURATION

The ASTC-ECU judges whether the brake is applied suddenly or not according to the stop lamp switch "ON" signal sent from the stop lamp switch and the frequency signal in proportion to the rotation speed of each wheel sent from the wheel speed sensors. If the ASTC-ECU judges that the brake is applied suddenly, it sends the ESS activation request signal to the ETACS-ECU. When the ETACS-ECU receives the ESS activation request signal, it makes the turn-signal lamps flash quickly.

Emergency stop signal operating condition


It operates when the following conditions are met.

  • The vehicle speed is 55 km/h or more.
  • The brake pedal is depressed, and the system judges that the brake is applied suddenly according to the deceleration of the vehicle and the ABS operation status.

Emergency stop signal operation stop condition


The operation will be stopped under one of the following conditions.

  • The brake pedal is released.
  • The hazard warning lamps are flashing by the hazard warning lamp switch operation.
  • The system judges that the brake is not applied suddenly according to the deceleration of the vehicle and the ABS operation status.

HILL DESCENT CONTROL (HDC)


System Configuration

The ASTC-ECU uses wheel speed sensor, longitudinal G sensor, and master cylinder fluid pressure signals as well as CAN communication to enable/disable the HDC. When you press the HDC switch with the HDC permission conditions satisfied, the ASTC-ECU will turn on the HDC (The HDC will not be turned on only when you turn the ignition switch from "LOCK" to "ON").

HDC switch

A HDC switch is located on the front floor console. When the driver presses this switch briefly, the HDC can be turned on and off. When the HDC is turned on, the HDC indicator lamp on the combination meter will illuminate.
The ASTC-ECU does not accept any signal from the HDC switch when you press and hold it for 15 seconds in order to avoid unintentional HDC operation. To resume (enable) the HDC, you should restart the engine.
When any of the following conditions is satisfied, the HDC will be disabled and the multi-information display informs the driver of it.

HDC set speed


The HDC set speed is memorised when any of the following conditions is met:

  • The HDC is activated with the accelerator and brake pedals released.
  • The driver has activated the HDC by depressing the accelerator pedal, and then released the pedal.
  • The driver has activated the HDC by depressing the brake pedal, and then released the pedal.
  • The driver depresses the accelerator pedal while the HDC is active, and then releases the pedal.
  • The driver depresses the brake pedal while the HDC is active, and then releases the pedal.

OFF-ROAD MODE


System Configuration

The ASTC-ECU detects slippage from the wheel speed sensor to control the engine, the transmission and the brake. In the off-road mode, you can obtain a maximum off-road performance by selecting an appropriate mode according to road conditions.

Off-road mode selector


A off-road mode selector is located on the front floor console. While the off-road mode is active, you can select an appropriate off-road mode option ("GRAVEL", "MUD/SNOW", "SAND", or "ROCK") by pressing the off-road mode selector briefly. The mode, which you select, will be displayed on the multi-information display.
The mode will be fixed to "GRAVEL" when you press and hold the off-road mode selector for 15 seconds. Then the ASTC-ECU does not accept any signal from the off-road mode selector to avoid an unintentional operation.
When any of the following conditions is satisfied. the off-road mode selector will be disabled and the multi-information display informs the driver of it.
Message on the multi-information display
 Vehicle status


The drive-mode selector is at "2H" or "4H".


Rear differential lock system is locked.



  • The brake pads are hot.
  • The ASTC-ECU detects a malfunction.

Operation




The ASTC-ECU will enter one of off-road modes when you select "4HLc" or "4LLc" by operating the drive-mode selector. After the system enters off-road mode, the ASTC-ECU activates the TCL system according to a wheel slippage and a current-selected mode.

Brake pad temperature detection


When the ASTC-ECU estimates a brake pad temperature, and determines that it is hot, the ECU will provide an acoustic and visual warning. When four seconds elapse with the brake pads hot, the ATC and the HDC will be disabled.

FAIL-SAFE DIAGNOSIS FUNCTION


ASTC-ECU monitors input and output signals any time and if any abnormality is detected in the system, fail signals are output to illuminate respective indicator lamp and provide control according to trouble symptoms as shown in the following table. (When ABS is faulty, the active stability control system and the active traction control system functions are disabled, but the other functions are not disabled when the active stability control system and the active traction control system are faulty. It should be noted that a method of illumination depends on the micro computer for monitoring.)
In addition, ASTC-ECU is equipped with the following functions to facilitate system inspections:

  • Diagnosis code output
  • Service data output
  • Actuator test
  • Freeze frame data output
All of the above-mentioned items can be tested by the M.U.T.-III.
