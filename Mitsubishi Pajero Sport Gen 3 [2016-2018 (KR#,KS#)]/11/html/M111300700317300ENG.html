INSPECTION


CYLINDER HEAD
1.Before cleaning the cylinder head, check it for traces of water and gas leakage and for cracks and any other damage.
2.Thoroughly remove oils, scale, sealants, carbon and other contamination. Clean the oil passages, then check using compressed air that they are not blocked.

caution The thickness of the metal that can be removed by grinding from both the cylinder head and the mating cylinder block is limited to 0.2 mm in total.
3.Check the cylinder head gasket surface for warp using a straightedge and thickness gauge. If the surface is warped beyond the limit, grind the surface for rectification.
Gasket surface warp
    Standard value: Less than 0.03 mm
    Limit: 0.2 mm
    Grinding limit: 0.2 mm
Cylinder head height
(standard value for new part):
119.9 - 120.1 mm

VALVES
1.Check the valve face for correct contact with the seat. Reface the valve if the contact is partial or one sided.

2.Replace the valve if its margin is smaller than the limit.
Standard values:
    Inlet 1.0 mm
    Exhaust 1.2 mm
Limits:
    Inlet 0.5 mm
    Exhaust 0.7 mm

3.Measure the total length of the valve. Replace the valve if the length is less than the limit.
Standard values:
    Inlet 112.30 mm
    Exhaust 114.11 mm
Limits:
    Inlet 111.80 mm
    Exhaust 113.61 mm

VALVE SPRINGS

1.Measure the free height of the spring. Replace the spring if its height is smaller than the limit.
Standard value: 50.8 mm
Limit: 49.8 mm
2.Measure the squareness of the spring. Replace the spring if it is out of square beyond the limit.
Standard value: 2° or smaller
Limit: 4°

VALVE GUIDE

Measure the valve guide inside diameter and valve stem diameter to calculate the clearance between the valve guide and valve stem. If the limit is exceeded, replace the valve guide or valve, or both.
Standard values:
    Inlet 0.02 - 0.05 mm
    Exhaust 0.04 - 0.07 mm
Limits:
    Inlet 0.10 mm
    Exhaust 0.15 mm

VALVE SEAT

Assemble the valve, then measure the contact width. If the measurement exceeds the specified limit, replace the valve seat.
Standard value: 0.9 - 1.3 mm
caution If the variation in the width exceeds 0.2 mm even if the contact width is within the standard value, replace or correct the valve sheet.

VALVE SEAT RECONDITIONING
1.Before reconditioning the valve seat, check the clearance between the valve guide and valve stem and, if necessary, replace the valve guide.

2.Resurface the valve seat to the indicated width and angles.
3.After resurfacing, lap the valve and valve seat using lapping compound. Then, check the valve stem projection (refer to VALVE SEATS in the INSPECTION section).

VALVE SEAT REPLACEMENT

1.Cut inside of the valve seat to be replaced until its wall becomes thin enough for removal, then remove the valve seat.

2.Rebore the valve seat hole in the cylinder head to a diameter matched to the diameter of the selected oversize valve seat.
Inlet valve seat hole diameter:
    0.3 oversize: 34.300 - 34.325 mm
Exhaust valve seat hole diameter:
    0.3 oversize: 31.800 - 31.825 mm
3.Before fitting the valve seat, cool it in liquid nitrogen to prevent damage to its hole in the cylinder head due to interference.
4.Resurface the valve seat. Refer to, the VALVE SEAT RECONDITIONING section.

VALVE GUIDE REPLACEMENT
1.Force out the valve guide toward the cylinder block using a press.
2.Machine the valve guide hole in the cylinder head to the size matched to the selected oversize valve guide.
caution Do not use a replacement valve guide of the same size as the removed one.
Valve guide hole diameters
    0.05 oversize: 11.050 - 11.068 mm

3.Press-fit the valve guide until it remains protruded above the cylinder head by the amount indicated in the drawing.
Protrusion: 13.7 - 14.3 mm
note Press the valve guide from above the cylinder head.
note The valve guides for the inlet valves are different in length from those for the exhaust valves (45.5 mm for inlet valves; 50.5 mm for exhaust valves)
4.After installing the valve guide, insert a new valve in it to check for smooth movement.
