Code No. P0333: Left Bank Detonation Sensor Circuit High Input


OPERATION



  • The sensor signal is input to the engine-ECU connector terminal K/S from the left bank detonation sensor.
  • The power voltage is earthed to the engine-ECU connector terminal K/SE from the left bank detonation sensor.

FUNCTION



  • The detonation sensor detects the vibration of the cylinder block caused by detonation waves, and inputs a signal to the engine-ECU.
  • In response to the signal, the engine-ECU provides controls to retard the ignition timing when the detonation occurs.

TROUBLE JUDGEMENT


Check Condition
  • More than 2 seconds have passed since the engine starting sequence was completed.
Judgement Criterion
  • Left bank detonation sensor output voltage is more than 2.25 V for 2 seconds.

FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION



  • Fix the ignition timing with an allowance against detonation.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Short circuit in left bank detonation sensor circuit or loose connector contact
  • Failed engine-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. Check of short to power supply in K/S line between left bank detonation sensor connector and engine-ECU connector.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.

STEP 2. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code

  • Reconfirmation of diagnosis code.

Q. Is diagnosis code set?
Replace the engine-ECU.
