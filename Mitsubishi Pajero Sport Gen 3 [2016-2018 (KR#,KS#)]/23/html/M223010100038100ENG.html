ELECTRONIC CONTROL SYSTEM


SYSTEM CONFIGURATION DIAGRAM


The system regulates gear shifting, lock-up clutch and clutch hydraulic pressure according to sensor signals. thus realising a smooth shifting.


ELECTRONIC COMPONENTS


INHIBITOR SWITCH

By using a combination of terminals in the position circuit, the inhibitor switch informs the A/T-ECU which AT range is engaged. The functions below are available:

  • Allows the engine to start only when the selector lever is placed at "P" or "N" rage (avoids unintended acceleration)
  • The inhibitor switch signal is used to control the gear shifting.

TRANSMISSION WIRING HARNESS

The transmission wiring harness functions as a centralised connector to the oil temperature sensor and the solenoids, and is secured on the transmission case.

OIL TEMPERATURE SENSOR

The oil temperature sensor, which is integrated in the transmission wiring harness, is secured on the valve body. This sensor detects the oil temperature in the hydraulic pressure control circuit directly, and sends a signal to the A/T-ECU accordingly. This allows the system to control the gear shifting, the lock-up clutch and the slippage in accordance with the oil temperature, thus alleviating rough shifting across the wide range of the oil temperature.

SHIFT SOLENOIDS (SR and SB)



The shift solenoids (SR and SB), which are installed on the valve body, are activated or deactivated by the A/T-ECU. These shift solenoids changes the shift gears and triggers the engine braking. Just if a fail-safe function detects that a shift solenoid fails, the A/T-ECU will deenergise the relevant solenoid.

PRESSURE CONTROL SOLENOIDS (SL1, SL2, SL3, SL4 and SL5)



The pressure control solenoids (SL1, SL2, SL3, SL4 and SL5), which are installed on the valve body, linearly control the hydraulic pressure in proportion to the output signals from the A/T-ECU. These solenoids linearly control the hydraulic pressure to the clutches (C1, C2, C3 and C4) and the brakes (B1) to achieve smooth gear changes.
These pressure controls solenoids allows the gear change from 1st to 8th gears.
Just if a fail-safe function detects that a pressure control solenoid fails, the A/T-ECU will deenergise the relevant solenoid.

LINE PRESSURE CONTROL SOLENOID (SLT)


The line pressure control solenoid (SLT) is installed on the valve body. The solenoid controls the line pressure linearly by adjusting the throttle oil pressure in accordance with a map stored in the A/T-ECU. The A/T-ECU determines the map in accordance with the throttle valve opening signal and the engine torque. Due to this, the system can control the oil pressure to the clutches and the brakes, thus achieving a smooth gear change.
Just if a fail-safe function detects that the line pressure control solenoid (SLT) fails, the A/T-ECU will deenergise the relevant solenoid. (If the line pressure control solenoid (SLT) is deenergised due to a malfunction other than seizure, the line pressure will reach its maximum.)

LOCKUP CONTROL SOLENOID (SLU)


The lock-up control solenoid (SLU) is installed on the valve body. The A/T-ECU energises the lock-up control solenoid (SLU) in accordance with engine speed, throttle signal, input shaft speed sensor (NT) signal and output shaft speed sensor (SP) signal. This allow the system to control the lock-up clutch oil pressure linearly. Due to this, the system controls the lock-up operation and the slippage in the torque converter.
Just if a fail-safe function detects that the lock-up control solenoid (SLU) fails, the A/T-ECU will deenergise the relevant solenoid.

INPUT SHAFT SPEED SENSOR (NT) AND OUTPUT SHAFT SPEED SENSOR (SP)


The input shaft speed sensor (NT) is integrated in the oil pump, and the output shaft speed sensor (SP) is integrated in the transmission.
The input shaft speed sensor detects the input shaft speed at the shaft rotor, and the output shaft speed sensor detects the speed of the rear ring gear. These sensors send signals to the A/T-ECU accordingly on a continuous basis.
Based on these signals, the A/T-ECU controls the engine torque, the gear change timing and the lock-up operation.

SPORT MODE CONTROL
A driver can select the optional gear level by shifting the selector lever from the "D" range to the sports mode and selecting + (upshift)/- (downshift). This provides the driver the sport driving with the manual-driving-feel. However, the lockup control is automatically made. Also the automatic shifting reactivates by operating the lever from the sport mode to the "D" range. At the sport mode control, the control mentioned below is different from the "D" range.
Auto downshift control
  • To ensure safe driving, the downshifting is automatically conducted when the vehicle speed becomes lower than the specified one.
Upshift permission control
  • To reduce transmission load, upshifting is possible only when the vehicle speed is higher than the specified one.
Downshift permission control
  • To prevent engine over rev, downshifting is possible only when the vehicle speed is lower than the specified one.

TORQUE REDUCTION CONTROL AND LINE PRESSURE CONTROL
The torque reduction control, which reduces the shift-shock by outputting the torque reduction request signal from the A/T-ECU to the engine-ECU and cutting the engine torque increase due to the shift change on shifting, has been adopted. In addition to the torque reduction control, the line pressure control which reduces the shift shock by controlling the line pressure has been adopted.
