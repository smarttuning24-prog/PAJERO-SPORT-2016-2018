Code No.P2762: Lock-up Control Solenoid System (Malfunction)


OPERATION



  • Solenoid closes or opens according to the signals from the A/T-ECU.
  • The A/T-ECU energises or deenergises solenoid, based on input signals such as accelerator pedal position sensor opening angle, inhibitor switch, etc.

DIAGNOSIS CODE SET CONDITIONS


If a status continues with which the lock-up solenoid current differs from the A/T-ECU target value, code No.P2762 is set.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Malfunction of valve body (Lock-up control solenoid)
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Malfunction of the A/T-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. Measure the resistance at A/T-ECU connector.
Disconnect the connector, and measure the resistance between terminal SLU line and terminal SLUG line at the harness side.
OK: 5.0 - 5.6 Ω (A/T fluid temperature 20°C)

OK: 5.0 - 5.6 Ω (A/T fluid temperature 20°C)

STEP 3. Measure the resistance at A/T-ECU connector.

  • Disconnect the connector, and measure the resistance between terminal SLU and body earth at the wiring harness side.
  • Disconnect the connector, and measure the resistance between terminal SLUG and body earth at the wiring harness side.
OK: No continuity

Go to Step 4.

STEP 4. Measure the resistance at transmission wiring harness connector.

  • Disconnect the connector, and measure the resistance between terminal SLU and body earth at the transmission assembly side.
  • Disconnect the connector, and measure the resistance between terminal SLUG and body earth at the transmission assembly side.
OK: No continuity

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Go to Step 6.

STEP 5. Check for open circuit, short circuit and harness damage in SLU, SLUG line between transmission wiring harness connector and A/T-ECU connector.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the connector(s) or the wiring harness.

STEP 6. Check for open circuit, short circuit and harness damage in SLU, SLUG line between transmission wiring harness connector and lock-up control solenoid connector.

Q. Is the check result normal?
