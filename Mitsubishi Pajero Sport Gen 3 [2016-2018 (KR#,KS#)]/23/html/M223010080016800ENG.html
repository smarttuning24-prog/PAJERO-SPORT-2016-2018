POWER TRAIN


The power train consists of 4 sets of clutches, 2 sets of brakes, 1 one-way clutch, and 3 sets of planetary gears.

POWER FLOW


1ST GEAR (D range)




1.The input shaft drives the front planetary carrier.
2.As the front sun gear is stationary, the front planetary carrier drives the front ring gear via the pinion gear.
3.As the C1 clutch is engaged, the front ring gear is engaged with the rear sun gear.
4.Torque is transferred from the rear sun gear through the short pinion gear to the long pinion gear.
5.As the one-way clutch F1 locks the rear planetary carrier, drive torque is transferred to the rear ring gear, thus driving the output shaft.

2ND GEAR (D range)




1.The input shaft drives the front planetary carrier.
2.As the front sun gear is stationary, the front planetary carrier drives the front ring gear via the pinion gear.
3.As the C1 clutch is engaged, the front ring gear is engaged with the rear sun gear.
4.Torque is transferred from the rear sun gear through the short pinion gear to the long pinion gear.
5.As the B1 brake locks the middle sun gear, the long pinion gear revolves around the middle sun gear, thus rotating the rear planetary carrier together with the rear ring gear.

3RD GEAR (D range)




1.The input shaft drives the front planetary carrier.
2.As the front sun gear is stationary, the front planetary carrier drives the front ring gear via the pinion gear.
3.As the C1 clutch is engaged, the front ring gear is engaged with the rear sun gear.
4.As the C3 clutch is engaged, the front ring gear is engaged with the middle sun gear. Torque is also transferred through this power flow route to the rear planetary gear assembly.
5.As the C1 and C3 clutches are engaged, the short pinion gear is engaged with the long pinion.
6.The rear planetary carrier rotates together with the rear sun gear and the middle sun gear.
7.The rear ring gear rotates.

4TH GEAR (D range)




1.The input shaft drives the front planetary carrier.
2.As the front sun gear is stationary, the front planetary carrier drives the front ring gear via the pinion gear.
3.As the C1 clutch is engaged, the front ring gear is engaged with the rear sun gear.
4.As the C4 clutch is engaged, the front planetary carrier is engaged with the middle sun gear. Torque is transferred through this power flow route to the rear planetary gear assembly.
5.At this time the rear sun gear rotates more slowly than the middle sun gear.
6.As a result, the short pinion gear and the middle pinion gear revolves around the middle sun gear, thus rotating the rear ring gear.

5TH GEAR (D range)




1.The input shaft drives the front planetary carrier.
2.As the front sun gear is stationary, the front planetary carrier drives the front ring gear via the pinion gear.
3.As the C1 clutch is engaged, the front ring gear is engaged with the rear sun gear.
4.As the C2 clutch is engaged, the rotation of the input shaft is transferred through the intermediate shaft to the rear planetary carrier. This power flow route also transfers drive torque to the rear planetary gear assembly.
5.The short pinion gear, which is engaged with the long pinion gear, rotates the rear ring gear together with the rear planetary carrier.

6TH GEAR (D range)




1.The input shaft drives the front planetary carrier.
2.As the C4 clutch is engaged, the front planetary carrier is engaged with the middle sun gear. Torque is transferred through this power flow route to the rear planetary gear assembly.
3.As the C2 clutch is engaged, the rotation of the input shaft is transferred through the intermediate shaft to the rear planetary carrier. This power flow route also transfers drive torque to the rear planetary gear assembly.
4.At this time, drive torque is also transferred via the middle sun gear to the rear planetary gear. Then it will be transferred via the rear planetary carrier to the short pinion gear and the long pinion gear.
5.The short pinion gear and long pinion gear, which now functions as one unit, rotates rear ring gear.

7TH GEAR (D range)




1.The input shaft drives the front planetary carrier.
2.As the front sun gear is stationary, the front planetary carrier drives the front ring gear via the pinion gear.
3.As the C3 clutch is engaged, the front ring gear is engaged with the middle sun gear. Torque is transferred through this power flow route to the rear planetary gear assembly.

8TH GEAR (D range)




1.As the C2 clutch is engaged, the rotation of the input shaft is transferred through the intermediate shaft to the rear planetary carrier.
2.As the B1 brake locks the middle sun gear, the long pinion gear revolves around the middle sun gear, thus rotating the rear ring gear together with the short pinion gear.
Note that the front planetary gear assembly has nothing to do with the torque transfer as the C1 and C3 clutches are disengaged.

REVERSE GEAR




1.The input shaft drives the front planetary carrier.
2.As the front sun gear is stationary, the front planetary carrier drives the front ring gear via the pinion gear.
3.As the C3 clutch is engaged, the front ring gear is engaged with the middle sun gear. Torque is transferred through this power flow route to the rear planetary gear assembly.
4.Drive torque is transferred via the middle sun gear to the long pinion gear.
5.As the B2 brake locks the rear planetary carrier, drive torque is transferred to the rear ring gear.
Note that the rear ring gear rotates in the opposite direction of engine rotation.
