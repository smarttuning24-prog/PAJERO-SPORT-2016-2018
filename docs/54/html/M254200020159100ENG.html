STRUCTURE





  • The system receives the following switch input signals through the hard wire, and then displays the corresponding information on the combination meter.

    • Door open/closed switch status
    • Hill descent control (HDC) switch status
    • Off-road mode switch status
  • The CAN bus line consists of two lines, CAN_L and CAN_H (CAN Low and CAN High, respectively), as well as two terminal resistors (A twisted-pair cable, highly resistant to noise, is used for the communications line).
  • The CAN bus line connecting the two terminal resistors is the main bus line, and the CAN bus line connecting each ECU is the stub line.
  • The terminal resistors are installed in the engine-ECU, ETACS-ECU, A/C-ECU and gateway-ECU to stabilize communication signals (The terminal resistance is set at approximately 120 Ω).
  • The following components are connected in the CAN bus line as follows.

    • ETACS-ECU
    • Combination meter
    • SRS-ECU
    • KOS&OSS-ECU <Vehicles with keyless operation system>
    • A/C-ECU <Vehicles with automatic A/C>
    • Transfer-ECU <Vehicles with 4WD>
    • Steering wheel sensor
    • electric parking brake ECU <Vehicles with electric parking brake system>
    • ABS or ASC-ECU
    • A/T-ECU <Vehicles with A/T>
    • Engine-ECU
    • FCM-ECU <Vehicles with forward collision mitigation system>
    • Headlamp automatic leveling-ECU <Vehicles with auto headlamp leveling system>
    • Camera-ECU <Vehicles with multi around monitor system>
    • Clearance warning control unit <Vehicles with ultrasonic misacceleration mitigation, blind spot warning or corner sensor system>
    • Gateway-ECU <Vehicles with automatic A/C>
    • Diagnosis connector
