The rear window defogger does not work. <vehicles with cooler>


COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM


If the rear window defogger does not work after switching on (the defogger timer will be on for approximately 20 minutes), the rear window defogger relay circuit may be defective.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Malfunction of the rear window defogger relay
  • Wiring harness or connector failure
  • Malfunction of heater control panel
  • Malfunction of ETACS-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code.


Check that the ETACS-ECU has not set a diagnosis code.

Q. Is the diagnosis code set?

Carry out the diagnosis code procedures. Refer to .

Go to Step 2.

STEP 2. M.U.T.-III other system’s diagnosis code.


Check that the engine-ECU has not set a diagnosis code.

Q. Is the diagnosis code set?

Troubleshoot the engine (Refer to GROUP 13A - Troubleshooting, Diagnosis Code Chart )

Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. M.U.T.-III data list.


Check the ETACS-ECU system data list.

  • Turn the rear window defogger switch from off to on.

STEP 4. Check the rear window defogger relay.


Refer to .

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 5.

Replace the defogger relay.

STEP 5. Voltage measurement at the rear window defogger connector (power supply terminal).


(1)Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Start the engine.
(3)Rear window defogger switch: ON.
(4)Measure the voltage between the rear window defogger connector (power supply terminal) and the body earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 6.

Go to Step 7.

STEP 6. Resistance measurement at the rear window defogger connector (earth terminal).


(1)Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Measure the resistance between the rear window defogger connector (earth terminal) and the body earth.
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 13.

Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness wires between rear window defogger connector terminal and body earth.

STEP 7. Voltage measurement at the rear window defogger relay connector (power supply terminal).


(1)Remove the relay, and measure at the junction block side.
(2)Measure the voltage between the rear window defogger relay connector (power supply terminal) and the body earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 9.

Go to Step 8.

STEP 8. Check of open circuit in power supply line between rear window defogger relay connector and fusible link.


Q. Is the check result normal?

Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).

Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.

STEP 9. Check of short to power supply, and open circuit in output line between rear window defogger relay connector and rear window defogger connector.


Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 10.

Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.

STEP 10. Voltage measurement at the rear window defogger relay connector (input terminal).


(1)Remove the relay, and measure at the junction block side.
(2)Ignition switch: ON.
(3)Measure the voltage between the rear window defogger relay connector (input terminal) and the body earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 12.

Go to Step 11.

STEP 11. Check of short to power supply, and open circuit in input line between rear window defogger relay connector and ignition switch (IG2).


Q. Is the check result normal?

Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).

Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.

STEP 12. Check of short to power supply, and open circuit in DEF1 line between rear window defogger relay connector and ETACS-ECU connector.


Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 13.

Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
