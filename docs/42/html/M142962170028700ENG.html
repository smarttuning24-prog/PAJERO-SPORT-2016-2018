Code No.B1137 P range detect SW <Vehicles with 4D5>


caution When replacing the ECU, always check that the communication circuit is normal.

DIAGNOSTIC FUNCTION


If an error is detected in the P range detection switch circuit system, KOS&OSS-ECU sets diagnosis code No.B1137, and displays the immobiliser certification system inspection warning the combination meter.

JUDGEMENT CRITERIA


Check Conditions
  • Power supply mode: ON
Judgement Criterion
  • When the vehicle speed is 10 km/h or more, 1 second or more has elapsed since the P range detection switch is detected to be OFF (selector lever is in P position).
  • When the P range detection switch is OFF, the signal other than selector lever P position is received for 2 seconds or more.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Damaged wiring harness or connector (short to power supply or open circuit in PDSW line)
  • Selector lever assembly malfunction (OFF seizure of P range detection switch)
  • Malfunction of the engine-ECU
  • Malfunction of the A/T-ECU
  • Malfunction of KOS&OSS-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. Check of selector lever assembly (P range detection switch)


Disconnect the selector lever assembly connector, and check the continuity at the equipment side.
Check item

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2.

Replace the selector lever assembly.

STEP 2. M.U.T.-III other system diagnosis code


Check the diagnosis code of engine-ECU or A/T-ECU.

Q. Is the diagnosis code set?

YES <Set in engine-ECU> : Troubleshoot the engine-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13E - Troubleshooting ).

YES <Set in A/T-ECU> : Troubleshoot the A/T-ECU (Refer to GROUP 23A - Troubleshooting).

NO : Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. Check of short to power supply or open circuit in PDSW line between selector lever assembly connector and KOS&OSS-ECU connector


note Because the conformity cannot be determined even when the digital tester is used to measure the voltage of PDSW line between the selector lever assembly connector and KOS&OSS-ECU connector, be sure to use the oscilloscope (Outputs the saw tooth waveform of 0 to 5 V with intervals of 10 ms.).

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 4.

Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.

Check the KOS&OSS-ECU data list.

Go to Step 5.

STEP 5. Check whether the diagnosis code is set again.


Q. Is the diagnosis code set?

Replace the KOS&OSS-ECU and register the chassis number.
