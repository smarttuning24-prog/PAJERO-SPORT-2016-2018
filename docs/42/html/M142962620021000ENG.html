Inspection Procedure 15: Vehicle power does not turn ON (Not possible with both of power supply mode ACC and ON).


caution Before replacing the ECU, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit and the communication circuit are normal.

COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM


When the vehicle power does not turn ON (not possible with both of power supply mode ACC and ON), the battery, CAN bus line, KOS&OSS-ECU, KOS&OSS-ECU power supply, or earth circuit may have a problem.
note KOS&OSS-ECU can communicate with the M.U.T.-III even when the engine switch is in the "OFF" position.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Malfunction of battery
  • Damaged wiring harness and connectors
  • Malfunction of engine switch (poor connector contact, poor engine switch contact)
  • Malfunction of the CAN bus line
  • Malfunction of KOS&OSS-ECU
  • Malfunction of the keyless operation key battery

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


note Prior to diagnosis, read "Troubleshooting procedure when vehicle power does not turn ON" .

STEP 1. Battery check


Perform the battery test to check the battery status (Refer to GROUP 54A - Battery ).

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2.

Replace the battery.

STEP 2. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics


Use the M.U.T.-III to diagnose the CAN bus lines.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 3.

Repair the CAN bus line (Refer to GROUP 54C - Troubleshooting ).

STEP 3. Check that the M.U.T.-III communicates with this system


Check that the communication with M.U.T.-III is possible.
(1)Turn on and off any of the door switches several times, or open any of the doors.
(2)Check that KOS&OSS-ECU can communicate with the M.U.T.-III.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 4.

Carry out the troubleshooting of KOS&OSS-ECU power supply or earth circuit malfunction (Refer to ).

STEP 4. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code


Check if the diagnosis code is set in the KOS&OSS-ECU.

Q. Is the diagnosis code set?

Refer to diagnosis code chart .

Go to Step 5.

STEP 5. Engine switch check


Continuity check of engine switch (Refer to ).

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 6.

Replace the engine switch.

STEP 6. Keyless operation key battery charged level check


Check whether the LED illuminates when the lock/unlock switch on the keyless operation key is pressed.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 7.

Replace the battery in the keyless operation key

STEP 7. Customised contents check


Use the customisation function to check the setting contents of "KOS feature" (Refer to ).
note If the customized contents do not satisfy your intention, customise the system again.

Q. Which content is selected?

Set to "Both enable" or "ENG start enable" : Go to Step 8.

"DoorEntry enable" or "Both disable" : Go to Step 10.

STEP 8. Check of the troubles


Check the condition of the keyless operation key when the vehicle power does not turn ON.

Q. What kinds of circumstances cause the vehicle power supply to remain off?

When the keyless operation key is inside the vehicle. : Go to Step 9.

When the keyless operation key (discharged battery) is inserted into the key box. : Go to Step 10.

STEP 9. Keyless operation system communication test


Using M.U.T.-III, perform the antenna communication test to check that the outside transmission antenna assembly communicates normally (Refer to ).

  • Inside transmission antenna assembly (front)
  • Inside transmission antenna assembly (rear)
OK: Normal values are displayed for all the items

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 11.

Go to Step 10.

STEP 10. Check with another registered keyless operation key.


Check whether the power supply mode can be switched by another registered keyless operation key.

Q. Can the power supply mode be switched?

It can be switched. : Go to Step 12.

It cannot be switched. : Go to Step 11.

There is no other registered keyless operation key. : Go to Step 12.

STEP 11. Connection check of commercial electric products


Check whether commercial electric products are connected.

Q. Are commercial electric products connected?

Disconnect the commercial electric products as they may be interfering with communication. Then go to Step 14.

Go to Step 14.

STEP 12. Keyless operation key check


Check whether the registered keyless operation key is used.

Q. Is the registered keyless operation key used?

Used. : Register the key ID and keyless operation key ID of the keyless operation key. Then go to Step 13.

Not used (The keyless operation key is not registered or the keyless operation key is also used for another vehicle). : Refer to the key ID registration procedure manual to register the key ID. If the key ID cannot be registered, replace the keyless operation key and register the key ID.

STEP 13. Check of the troubles


Check that the vehicle power can be turned ON.

Q. Is the check result normal?

This diagnosis is complete.

Go to Step 14.

STEP 14. Check of the troubles


Check that the vehicle power can be turned ON.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Aggravation of radio wave condition caused by commercial electric products or intermittent malfunction is suspected (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).

Go to Step 15.

STEP 15. Check of the troubles


Check that the vehicle power can be turned ON or the indicator of engine switch in orange, green.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 16.

Replace the KOS&OSS-ECU and register the chassis number (Refer to ).

STEP 16. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code


Check if the diagnosis code is set in the KOS&OSS-ECU.

Q. Is the diagnosis code set?

Refer to diagnosis code chart .

This diagnosis is complete.
