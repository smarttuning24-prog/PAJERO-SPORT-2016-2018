Code No. C1222: Wheel SPD.SNSR.power circuit
Code No. C1225: Deviation between wheel speed


caution
  • If there is any problem in the CAN bus lines, an incorrect diagnosis code may be set. Prior to this diagnosis, diagnose the CAN bus lines (Refer to GROUP 54C - Troubleshooting ).
  • Whenever ECU is replaced, ensure that the CAN bus lines are normal.
  • When the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU) is replaced, always carry out the calibration of the steering wheel sensor and the G sensor (Refer to ).

OPERATION



  • A toothed rotor (for wheel speed detection) generates a voltage pulse as it moves across the pickup field of each wheel speed sensor.
  • The wheel speed sensor transmit the frequency of the pulses to the ASTC-ECU.

DIAGNOSIS CODE SET CONDITIONS


The ASTC-ECU monitors the signals from each wheel speed sensor. If any faults below is found in these sensor signals, the ECU will set the relevant diagnosis code. When the abnormal wheel is identified, diagnosis code No. C1201, C1206, C1211, or C1216 is set.

  • Missing sensor signal
  • Sensor signal, which will not be created under normal operation
  • Unfeasible difference among the wheel speed sensor signals
  • When the ABS/ASTC control is being activated for one minute or more.

PROBABLE CAUSES


Current trouble
  • Improper installation of the wheel speed sensor
  • Malfunction of the wheel speed sensor
  • Wheel bearing malfunction
  • Excessive gap between the wheel speed sensor and the rotor (for wheel speed detection)
  • Adhesion of foreign materials on the rotor (for wheel speed detection)
  • Deformation of the rotor (for wheel speed detection)
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Malfunction of the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU)
Past trouble
  • The following conditions may occur.

    • Either of the right or left wheel is turning, or either of the front or rear wheel are turning.
    • Unstable vehicle attitude
    • External noise interference
    • The vehicle is driven on snowy or rough road.

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnosis
Use the M.U.T.-III to diagnose the CAN bus lines.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair the CAN bus line (Refer to GROUP 54C - Troubleshooting ). On completion, go to Step 2.

STEP 2. Diagnosis code recheck after resetting CAN bus lines

Q. Is the relevant diagnosis code No. C1222 or C1225 set?
Go to Step 3.
This diagnosis is complete.

STEP 3. Check whether the wheel speed sensors are installed properly.
Check the mounting status of wheel speed sensors (sensor dislocation, mounting bolt loosening, etc.) for all wheels.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Install the wheel speed sensor properly (Refer to ).

STEP 4. Check the wheel speed sensor.
Refer to .

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Replace the wheel speed sensor.

STEP 5. Check for wheel bearing looseness
note The wheel bearing looseness is suspected to be the cause of excessive gap between the wheel speed sensor and rotor. Therefore, check the wheel bearing looseness of each wheel.

  • Front: Refer to GROUP 26 - On-vehicle Service .
  • Rear: Refer to GROUP 27 - On-vehicle Service .

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
NO <when the relevant wheel is front> : Replace the wheel bearing (Refer to GROUP 26 - Front Axle Hub Assembly ).
NO <when the relevant wheel is rear> : Replace the wheel bearing (Refer to GROUP 27 - Axle Shaft Assembly ).

STEP 6. Check of the rotor (for wheel speed detection)
Check the rotor (for wheel speed detection) for adhesion of foreign materials or deformation.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
NO <when the relevant wheel is front> : Remove the foreign materials and clean the rotor. If deformation is found, replace the wheel bearing (Refer to GROUP 26 - Front Axle Hub Assembly ).
NO <when the relevant wheel is rear> : Remove the foreign materials and clean the rotor. If deformation is found, replace the wheel bearing (Refer to GROUP 27 - Axle Shaft Assembly ).

(3)Ignition switch: "ON"
(4)Measure the voltage between the power supply terminal as well as signal terminal of wheel speed sensor circuit and the body earth.

STEP 14. Diagnosis code recheck


Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1)Erase the diagnosis code.
(2)Ignition switch: LOCK (OFF) position to ON
(3)Drive the vehicle until the vehicles speed reaches 20 km/h.

Q. Is diagnosis code No. C1222 or C1225 set?
Replace the wheel speed sensor (Refer to ). Then go to Step 15.
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).

Check again if the diagnosis code is set.

(2)Ignition switch: LOCK (OFF) position to ON
(3)Drive the vehicle until the vehicles speed reaches 20 km/h.
