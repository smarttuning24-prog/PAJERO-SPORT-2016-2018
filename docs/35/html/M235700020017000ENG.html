GENERAL INFORMATION


Active Stability & Traction Control system (ASTC) is available as optional equipment.

  • ASTC is a system combining the functions of Active Stability Control system (ASC) and Active Traction Control system (ATC).
  • When ATC detects the slip of the driving wheel (ex. during startup on low µ road), it automatically applies the brakes to the slipping driving wheel. At the same time, ATC reduces the engine output and prevents the wheel spin when it determines that the engine torque is too high for the road surface µ.
  • When the ASC determines that the vehicle is in a dangerous condition, it reduces the engine output and applies brake force to four wheels independently to control the vehicle behaviour, avoiding the critical state.
  • Ensuring braking performance and improvement in service quality by fail-safe function during ASC system malfunction
  • The Emergency Stop Signal system (ESS), which makes the hazard warning lamps flash quickly when the brake is applied suddenly, has been adopted.
  • The Hill Start Assist (HSA) function has been adopted to hold and prevent the roll back of the vehicle when the vehicle is on a slope and the foot is transferred from the brake pedal to the accelerator pedal.
  • The Brake Assist (BA) control determines an emergency braking and increases the braking force for the drivers who cannot depress the brake sufficiently when the emergency brake is applied. This shortens the distance to stop or reduces the collision speed.
  • When the Trailer Stability Assist (TSA) detects an rolling, which is caused by the trailer vehicle during towing, the system controls the engine output and the road wheel brakes to stabilise the vehicle attitude.
  • A hill decent control has been introduced. This system will assist the driver in maintaining a stable vehicle speed by applying an automatic braking when he/she drives the vehicle downhill on a steep slope.
  • The driver can select an appropriate road condition (off-road mode) to allow the ABS and the ASTC to operate according to current road condition.
  • The forward collision mitigation system (FCM) brakes the vehicle automatically by controlling the brake fluid pressure in order to reduce injury or damage due to collision accident or avoid a collision itself.
note On vehicles with ASC, the ASTC-ECU controls ABS as well.
