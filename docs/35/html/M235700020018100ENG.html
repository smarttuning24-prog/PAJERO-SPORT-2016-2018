GENERAL INFORMATION


caution
  • In the following situations, the system provides neither control nor alarming.

    1. The vehicle in front of your vehicle is moving at a speed much lower than your vehicle’s speed.
    2. When a vehicle suddenly cuts in front of your vehicle.
    3. When the distance from a vehicle in front is extremely short.
    4. To an oncoming vehicle.
    5. When your vehicle’s speed is much lower.
    6. When the selector lever is in the "P" (PARK) or the "R" (REVERSE) position.

  • The forward collision warning function and/or the FCM braking function may not activate in the following situations.

    1. When a motorcycle, bicycle, pedestrian, animal, fallen objects, or anything other than a vehicle is present in front of your vehicle.
    2. When a vehicle suddenly appear just in front of your vehicle.
    3. When a vehicle cuts in front of your vehicle very closely.
    4. When the vehicle in front is offset to the left or right.
    5. When the vehicle in front is towing a trailer.
    6. A freight trailer that is not carrying a container.
    7. The vehicle has a protruding load from the carrier.
    8. The vehicle has a low vehicle height.
    9. The vehicle has an extremely high ground clearance.
    10. When there is a different object near the vehicle.
    11. When driving on a road with many and successive curves, including when passing their entrances and outlets.
    12. When accelerating and decelerating quickly.
    13. When the system recognises driver’s steering, accelerating, braking or gear shifting actions as evasive actions to avoid collision.
    14. When driving on a road with steep alternating up and down slopes.
    15. When a road surface is surging, and there is unevenness.
    16. When driving on a road surrounded by a tall wall etc.
    17. When being driven in a tunnel.
    18. When the your vehicle changed lanes, and your vehicle approached immediately behind the vehicle in front.
    19. During a certain time after your vehicle turns left or right.
    20. When you are carrying extremely heavy loads in the rear seats or luggage area.
    21. After the engine has been running for an extended period of time.
    22. Under adverse weather conditions (rain, snow, sand storms, etc.)
    23. When the emblem on the front bumper and the sensor become dirty or adhering snow and ice.
    24. When water, snow or sand on the road are thrown up by the vehicle in front or an oncoming vehicle.
    25. An extremely strong electromagnetic wave is received from a source such as an illegal radio set, a spark from a wire, or a radar station.

  • The FCM control and alarm functions maybe triggered in the following situations.

    1. When there is a structure beside the entrance of a curve and intersection.
    2. When running on a narrow iron bridge.
    3. When passing through a gate with small head or side clearances.
    4. When there are metallic objects, steps or projections on the road surface.
    5. When quickly approaching a vehicle in front to overtake it.
    6. When passing an electronic toll collection gate.
    7. When running under an overpass, an pedestrian overpass or a tunnel.
    8. When running in Multi-storey car park.
    9. When running the road the gradient changes suddenly.
    10. When stopping very close to a wall or a vehicle in front.
    11. When passing close to the vehicle or an object.
    12. When driving on the road that the vehicle in front runs in offset position from your vehicle.
    13. When the course of the vehicle is overgrown with weeds.

  • Turn off the system beforehand when the vehicle is placed in any of the following situations as the system can operate unexpectedly.

    1. When your vehicle’s wheels are driven by the engine on a lift.
    2. When your vehicle is towed or your vehicle tows another vehicle.
    3. When your vehicle is carried on a truck.
    4. When making sports driving on a circuit.
    5. When your vehicle is on a chassis dynamometer or free rollers.
    6. When the tyre pressure is not proper.
    7. When tyre chains are attached.

  • Improperly handling the system components may result in such problems as degraded sensor performance and ultimately in an accident.
  • Be sure to take the following precautions to keep the system in good operating conditions.

    1. Avoid application of strong shock loads to the sensor and never tamper with or remove the emblem and the sensor mounting screws.
    2. The emblem and the sensor should be kept clean.
    3. Do not cover the sensor mounted area of the vehicle or the emblem on the front bumper with a sticker or anything such as a grill guard etc.
    4. The emblem and the sensor should not be modified or painted.
    5. Avoid using different size tyres from those specified and maintain even tyre wear.
    6. Do not modify the suspension of your vehicle.
    7. Never spray or splash water on the emblem on the front bumper and the sensors.
If the Forward Collision Mitigation (FCM) determines that a collision may occur by using its integrated radar, it will provide a visual and audible warning on the combination meter to alert the driver of an impending danger, and controls the vehicle braking by increasing the brake fluid pressure or enhancing the brake pedal effort.
If a front-end collision still remains imminent despite of the warning above, the system will "jerk" the vehicle by applying an automatic braking briefly and inform the driver of an impending danger.If a collision is highly anticipated, the system will brake the vehicle automatically in order to avoid or reduce the injury or damage due to the collision accident.
note The warning timing can be switched between "FAR", "MIDDLE" and "NEAR" by operating the forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch.

FCM-ECU
 8
  • Receives forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch signal from the ASTC-ECU via CAN to perform the followings:

    • Switches on and off the FCM.
    • Switches the warning timing of the forward collision warning.
  • Switches the FCM and/or the warning timing in accordance with the forward collision mitigation system (FCM) and Ultrasonic misacceleration Mitigation System ON/OFF switch status, which is received from the ASTC-ECU via CAN communication.
  • Determines whether the FCM should be activated in accordance with the radar detection (distance or relative velocity) or the other vehicle information, which is received via CAN communication.
  • Sends a FCM brake jerk request, a FCM brake system prefill request, a FCM brake assist request, a FCM brake request and/or the target deceleration during the automatic braking via CAN communication to the ASTC-ECU.
  • Sends FCM status information and a visible and audible warning request to the combination meter via CAN communication.
ASTC-ECU
 9
  • Controls the brake fluid pressure according to the brake control request and the target deceleration speed, which is received from the FCM-ECU via CAN communication.
  • Activate the stop lamp illumination circuit when the automatic braking is in progress.
  • Performs a forward collision mitigation brake system prefill control according to the request, which is received from the FCM-ECU.
  • Activates the warning brake jerk, the brake assist and the automatic braking in accordance with the request from the FCM-ECU.
ETACS-ECU
 10
  • Contains the stop lamp activation circuit when the FCM automatic braking is in progress.
  • Sends a signal, which indicates stop lamp switch status via hardwires, to the CAN bus lines.
