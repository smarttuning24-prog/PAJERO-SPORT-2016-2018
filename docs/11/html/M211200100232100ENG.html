PISTON RELATION




1.PISTON

  • The pistons are made of a special aluminium alloy and the pistons have special resin coating on the skirt portion for low friction and seizure resistance.
    The shape of combustion chamber is optimized for the consisting of both engine performance and emission performance.
2.PISTON PIN

  • The full floating type of piston pin is used to improve the reliability by reducing the friction.
3.CONNECTING ROD

  • The connecting rods are made of highly rigid, forged carbon steel. The rod portion has a I-shaped cross section, and the small end is adopted step-shaped.
    The oil holes, which feed oil from the main journals of the crankshaft to the crankshaft pins, lubricate the bearings at the big ends of the connecting rods.
4.PISTON RING

  • The piston rings consist of No.1 ring, No.2 ring and Oil ring.
    The surface treatment increases longevity of the piston rings. The low tension piston rings reduce the friction.
5.CONNECTING ROD BEARING

  • The upper and lower connecting rod bearings are exclusively designed.
    The upper bearing consists of three layers (overlay, copper alloy, and steel plate).
    The lower bearing consists of two layers (aluminium alloy and steel plate).
    In addition, these bearings have the special grooves to improve the robustness toward foreign materials.
    The selection method is used to be appropriate for the clearance.

CRANKSHAFT RELATION




1.CRANKSHAFT

  • A forged crankshaft has been adopted.
    It has 5 main bearings and 8 balance weights.
    The crankshaft pins are located at equal 180° intervals.
    The oil holes feed engine oil from the journals to the pins.
    At the front crankshaft, the crankshaft gear, oil pump drive shaft and crankshaft pulley are tightened together with the crankshaft bolts.
2.CRANKSHAFT BEARING

  • The upper crankshaft bearings have oil grooves and the lower crankshaft bearings do not have oil grooves.
    Their bearing consist of three layers (overlay, aluminium alloy and steel plate). The overlay is adopted solid lubrication material to reduce friction.
3.THRUST BEARING

  • The thrust bearing, which sustains the load in the thrust direction, is provided at each end of the No. 3 bearing.
4.CRANKSHAFT PULLEY

  • The torsional damper reduces the crankshaft torsional vibration and greatly eliminates noise-producing torsional vibrations in the high speed range.
    The first pulley groove (5 ribs) and the second pulley groove (6 ribs) are provided for the V rib belt respectively.
    The timing mark is notched on the pulley flange to check the injection timing.
5.CRANKSHAFT SENSING RING
The crankshaft sensing ring is assembled to the crankshaft.
6.FLYWHEEL <M/T>

  • The flywheel is dual mass type.
7.DRIVE PLATE <A/T>

  • The drive plate is ring gear type.

VALVE RELATION




The valve train is the 4-valve, double overhead camshaft (DOHC) type in which the camshafts are located above the valves.
Two inlet valves and two exhaust valves for each cylinder are arranged in a V shape.
Comparing with the OHV (overhead valve), the valves are suitable for the high speed engine equipped with the MIVEC because the mass of the valve open and close system is small.
1.CAMSHAFT
2.ROCKER ARM
3.VALVE

  • The valves are heat resistant steel and a nitride coating is done to all the surfaces.
4.VALVE STEM SEAL

  • The valve stem seal employs springs to enhance sealing performance, minimizing oil passing down to the port.
5.VALVE SPRING

  • To prevent the engine from surging at high speeds, unequal-pitch springs are used.

MIVEC (MITSUBISHI INNOVATIVE VALVE TIMING ELECTRONIC CONTROL SYSTEM)




The 4N1 engine employs the variable valve timing system (MIVEC).
The MIVEC is the switching type variable system, which varies the timing and lift of the intake valves according to the engine speeds.
At low speeds, one of the two intake valves is lowered to strengthen the swirl force, resulting in the stable combustion.The both of valves are closed around at bottom dead centre to ensure the actual compression ratio and the startability at low temperatures.
At high speeds, the both valves are operated in the high lift range and the wide open angle to increase the charging effect.
In addition to the low lift cam and the rocker arm which drive the both intake valves respectively, the swinging T lever following to the high lift cam is arranged.
At low speeds, the T lever wing strikes nothing but air.Each valve is driven by low lift cam respectively. At high speeds, the oil pressure moves the piston of the rocker arm. The T lever pushes the rocker arm. The high lift cam drives the both valves.
The oil pressure supplied from the cylinder block enters the oil feeder control valve (OCV) built in the filter. The OCV switches the oil pressure when the speed is higher than the specified speed, and then controls the high/low speed switch.

CAMSHAFT




The camshaft is made hollow for weight reduction.
The cam position sensing ring for detecting the cam position (used by the cam position sensor) is press-fitted into the rear of inlet camshaft.

OIL FEEDER CONTROL VALVE




The oil feeder control valve employs the electromagnetic valve switching the oil pressure of the rocker arm and the rocker shaft which are affected by the MIVEC.This valve is actuated by the signal from the engine ECU.
