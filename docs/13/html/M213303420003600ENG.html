MIVEC (Mitsubishi Innovative Valve Electronic Control System)


Engine-ECU turns the oil feeder control valve ON/OFF according to engine speed in order to control oil pressure acting on the piston in the rocker arm. Thus, change is done between low-speed cam and high-speed cam.
System Configuration Diagram

Engine-ECU turns OFF (Duty: 0 %) the oil feeder control valve at low engine speed (approximately 2,300 r/min or less). As a result, oil pressure does not act on the piston in the rocker arm and inlet valve is driven by the low speed cam. Engine-ECU turns ON (Duty: For 2 seconds after change 100 %; after 2 seconds 60 %) the oil feeder control valve at high engine speed (approximately 2,300 r/min or more). As a result, oil pressure acts on the piston in the rocker arm and inlet valve is driven by the high speed cam. It will be continually driven by the low speed cam under following conditions.

  • Engine coolant temperature is low temperature.
  • For 2 seconds after engine is fully started.

Low engine speed

By reducing the valve opening time and swiftly closing the valve, the amount of intake air blowing back is restricted. At low speeds, the lift amount of one side of the intake valve is reduced to produce the swirl flow.
 The startability is improved. The exhaust gas is reduced and the low speed torque is improved.
High engine speed

Valve opening time is increased to increase input air volume.
 Improve output power
