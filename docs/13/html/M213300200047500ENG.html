INJECTOR

The injector is a solenoid control type that injects fuel in accordance with the injection signals sent by the engine-ECU. An injector, which is provided for each cylinder, is mounted in the cylinder head.
The injection characteristics of injectors vary individually. Therefore, a 30-digit alphanumeric ID code is stamped on each injector. Engine-ECU provides a fuel injection amount that suits the individual injection characteristics, based on these ID codes.

Structure and system
An injector consists of a nozzle, command piston, command chamber, and solenoid valve that controls the ON/OFF of the outflow orifice. The command piston moves vertically in accordance with the fuel pressure in the command chamber. As the nozzle is integrated with the command piston, the orifice of the injector opens and closes with the vertical movement of the command piston.


When the solenoid valve is OFF, the two-way valve pushes down, causing the outflow orifice of the command chamber to close. Because the command chamber fills with pressurised fuel, the nozzle pushes down together with the command piston. As a result, no injection of fuel takes place.
When the solenoid valve is ON, the two-way valve pulls up, causing the outflow orifice of the command chamber to open. The pressure in the command chamber decreases, causing the nozzle to pull up together with the command piston. This results in the injection of fuel.
When the solenoid valve is turned OFF, the two-way valve closes, immediately pushing down the nozzle and closing the fuel injection orifice. An injector can perform these ON/OFF operations within an extremely short length of time. Therefore, as the solenoid valve is turned ON/OFF two or more times, the injector can inject fuel in multiple stages according to the number of times it is turned ON/OFF.


THROTTLE VALVE CONTROL SERVO

A throttle valve control servo is installed in throttle body.
The throttle valve control servo performs the Open/Close of the throttle valve through the reduction gear.
Engine-ECU changes current direction according to the Open/Close direction and also changes current to the motor coil in order to the control throttle valve control servo.
Throttle valve control servo is composed of a good response, low energy, and small DC motor with brush and can generate rotation force corresponding to the current applied on the coil.


VARIABLE GEOMETRY CONTROL SOLENOID VALVE

The variable geometry control solenoid valve regulates the vacuum pressure sent to the variable geometry actuator from the vacuum pump based on the balance of magnetic and pneumatic force.
When the amount of driving current is large, the magnetic force is strong and the vacuum pressure sent to the variable geometry actuator increases. This allows the variable geometry actuator to operate.
When the amount of driving current is small, the magnetic force is weak and the vacuum pressure sent to the variable geometry actuator decreases. This allows the variable geometry actuator not to operate. Changing the amount of driving current on the solenoid valve in accordance with the engine operation, the engine-ECU controls the vacuum pressure sent to the variable geometry actuator.

OIL FEEDER CONTROL VALVE

An oil feeder control valve is installed on the cylinder head. The oil feeder control valve is a duty control type solenoid valve and is used in oil pressure control for cam change. When no current is passing through the oil feeder control valve’s coil (duty: 0 %), port B gets closed and the oil pressure path between port A and port C connects. Oil that had been acting on the cam change control piston is expelled from port C via port A. Thus, the low-speed cam drives the inlet valve. When current is passed through the coil (duty: 100 %) and the spool moves, port A gets closed and port B and port C get connected. At this time the oil pressure-fed from the oil pump passes from port B via port C and acts on the cam change control piston. Thus, high-speed cam drives the inlet valve. Engine-ECU changes the duty ratio and switches the cam in accordance with the engine operation.


ALTERNATOR G TERMINAL
The engine-ECU uses the ON/OFF duty signal of the alternator G terminal to control the alternator output voltage. When the engine-ECU decreases the ON duty, the alternator output voltage should decrease. At that time, the current should not flow from the alternator because the alternator output voltage becomes lower than the voltage of the charged battery. By this means, the battery charge can be controlled. When the engine-ECU increases the ON duty, the alternator output voltage should increase. At that time, the current should flow from the alternator because the alternator output voltage becomes higher than the voltage of the charged battery. By this means, the battery can be charged. In case the electric load is generated suddenly, the engine-ECU should control the alternator G terminal’s On-duty and should limit the sudden increase in the alternator load due to the generation. Thus the engine-ECU prevents the change in the idle speed. The engine-ECU charges the battery at the high voltage level by maximizing the ON duty when the speed decreases.
